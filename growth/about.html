Growth dashboard — how it works

This dashboard exists to answer one question first: are we growing the newsletter? Everything else — traffic, campaign performance, mentions — is in service of that.

The 90-day signup chart

Green bars are new signups per day; the magenta line is unsubscribes per day; blue dots at the top mark days a Vital City post was published. The chart makes the relationship between content and signups visible — articles that spike the green bars tend to be the ones worth doing more of.

Important nuance: daily signups are derived from the merged subscriber dataset. That dataset rebuilds once a day at 11:00 UTC, so the most recent 1–2 days may understate signups until the next rebuild. The Mailchimp /activity endpoint's own subs count drastically undercounts signups (it only sees direct-Mailchimp form signups, while most VC signups arrive via the Ghost form), which is why the dashboard uses the merged dataset instead.

Recent newsletter sends

The last 12 sent campaigns with subject, recipient count, open rate, click rate and unsubscribe count. Open rate cell colors: green ≥35%, blue 22–35%, orange below. (Be aware that Apple Mail's "Mail Privacy Protection" auto-opens inflate open rates across the industry; click rate is the more honest engagement number.)

Content cadence

Posts published in the last 90 days, with quick counts for the 7- and 30-day windows. Use this to see whether the publishing rhythm is steady — gaps in publishing usually show up later as flat green bars on the signup chart.

Subscriber quality

Two views of the engaged half of the list:

  • Rating distribution — Mailchimp's 1–5 star score, weighted by open rate and tenure. 4–5★ are people you can count on. A list mostly stuck at 2★ usually means many subscribers are new (no history yet) — which can be healthy.
  • Top email domains — where subscribers' addresses live. Useful for guessing who reads on Apple Mail (auto-opens skew the rate), and for spotting institutional clusters (.edu, .gov).

Active subscribers (MAU and AAU)

The headline KPI row includes two "active subscribers" tiles:

  • Active subscribers · 30d (MAU) — the count of unique people who opened at least one Vital City newsletter in the last 30 days. Not a proxy or a rating estimate: it's the actual union of email addresses from Mailchimp's per-campaign open data across every regular send in the window, deduped. The percentage next to the count is MAU as a share of the current list.
  • Active subscribers · 1y (AAU) — the same calculation across the last 365 days. The gap between MAU and AAU is your "lapsed but reachable" audience — people who used to engage but haven't recently. Re-engagement campaigns target exactly that gap.

Two caveats baked into the tooltips: Apple Mail's privacy auto-opens inflate this number across the industry (since the mail client opens emails on the user's behalf to fetch tracking pixels), so MAU/AAU should be read as a ceiling not a floor. And A/B-test ("variate") campaigns are excluded from the union because Mailchimp tracks openers against the child A/B sub-campaigns rather than the parent; the dashboard shows how many variate sends were skipped if that matters.

Editing the media-mentions whitelist

The "Media mentions" sidebar pulls from Google News with site-restricted queries — one query per outlet for the exact phrase "Vital City", one for @vitalcitynyc, and one for any vitalcitynyc.org URL share. This means we only see mentions from outlets we've named in the whitelist; the trade-off is much higher precision (we don't get noise from random sites that use the generic phrase) for slightly narrower coverage.

Adding or removing an outlet is a one-line edit to growth_pull.py, the file that runs in the daily refresh:

  1. Open growth_pull.py on GitHub: vitalcity-nyc/vital-city-catalogue → growth_pull.py.
  2. Click the pencil ✏️ icon to edit in the browser, then find the MENTION_OUTLETS list near the top.
  3. Each line is a tuple: ("domain.com", "Display Name"). To add: copy a line, change the values. To remove: delete the line. To rename for display only: change the second value.
  4. Scroll down and click Commit changes. The next 11 UTC refresh will pick it up. To trigger sooner, go to Actions → network-refresh → Run workflow.

What domain to use: the bare hostname without www. works for most outlets (e.g. nytimes.com). For Substacks, use the publication-specific subdomain (johnkroman.substack.com). When in doubt, try the search yourself first at news.google.com with the query "Vital City" site:DOMAIN — if it returns relevant results, the domain works.

Note on WNYC: WNYC's content frequently mentions Vital City on radio segments, but those audio pieces don't get indexed by Google News (only the show-page transcripts do, when they exist). Most contemporary WNYC + Vital City content surfaces under the gothamist.com domain instead — Gothamist and WNYC share a newsroom (both belong to New York Public Radio), and Gothamist publishes web write-ups for many WNYC stories. So the dashboard relies on the Gothamist search to catch most WNYC-adjacent mentions, and the wnyc.org search catches only the small subset of show pages that explicitly print the phrase. The 2022 launch coverage from "All of It" and "Brian Lehrer" lives there; current radio appearances may be missed.

Media / social mentions (the sidebar)

Two free feeds, both narrowed to NYC context so "Vital City" the brand isn't confused with the generic phrase:

  • Google News — external publications quoting "Vital City". Self-references are filtered out, and items must actually contain the brand name (not just link to the site).
  • Reddit — threads naming Vital City, mostly in r/nyc. These are usually shares of VC articles by users — useful signal of organic distribution.
  • Google Trends — an embedded interactive widget showing search interest for "Vital City" + NYC disambiguators over the last 12 months. Search interest is relative (0–100 = peak in the window), so use it for shape not absolute volume. Spikes typically trail by 2–7 days after a piece breaks through, so it's useful as a "did people go looking for us?" signal after coverage or a viral post.

X (Twitter) and Instagram numbers now live in their own card in the main column — see the "Social" section.

How often it refreshes

Once a day at 11:00 UTC (≈ 7am ET in summer, 6am in winter). The same GitHub Action that updates the Contact tool also runs growth_pull.py → encrypt_growth.py and pushes the new encrypted file. The "Last refresh" timestamp at the top of the dashboard tells you exactly when this snapshot was taken.

Confidentiality

This page uses the same passphrase as the Contact tool and the same end-to-end encryption (AES-256-GCM, PBKDF2-SHA256 600k rounds). The dashboard contains aggregate metrics but no PII; it's password-protected anyway to keep our growth numbers internal.
